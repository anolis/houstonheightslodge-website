A Letter from Our Lodge

Dear Friend,

Our Lodge is preparing to host our popular SausageFest on September 23rd, 2023 in the Houston Heights. Our lodge is dedicated to serving the Heights and Greater Houston Area through scholarship and service to the local community. 

We are able to offer scholarships to the community. All students are eligible to receive an application. We fund these scholarships through hosting fundraising events such as the SausageFest, ChiliCookoff, yard sales and various other social gatherings. One of our goals is to expand the scholarship program both in quantity and amount, however, we are in need of substantially more funding to meet this goal. 

Our organization was founded as a fraternal 501(c)8 organization dedicated to improving and elevating the character of humankind; to aiding our community and the world; to aiding the less fortunate; supporting the youth of our community; aiding our own members in times of distress; to supporting science and research foundations for the elimination of diseases and poverty; and to supporting the betterment of our community in every way possible. We live by our bywords are Friendship, Love and Truth (F, L & T). 

We believe that the best sponsor has values and principles that align with our organization and we would like to invite your company to have the opportunity to co-sponsor the event. We have limited spaces for sponsorship as we believe in developing a strong and genuine commitment with our sponsors.

Regards,


Independent Order of Oddfellows Heights Lodge #225
ABOUT 
Independent Order of Oddfellows #225

The Independent Order of the Oddfellows was founded in 1819 in Maryland, as it grew in popularity our IOOF lodge #225 in the Houston Heights was founded in 1923. This Oddfellows Lodge is the oldest and only surviving lodge in the Greater Houston area.  The Oddfellows are an organization dedicated to "visiting the sick, relieving the distress, burying the dead and educating the orphans". 

Odd Fellowship became the 1st national fraternity to include both men and women when it adopted the beautiful Rebekah Degree in 1851. This degree is based on the teachings found in the Holy Bible. Odd Fellows and Rebekahs were also the first fraternal organization to establish homes for our senior members and for orphaned children.

Today, Odd Fellows continue to exist with nearly 10,000 lodges in approximately 26 countries consisting of men and women who united together for mutual aid and conviviality, providing social and practical support for each other and their communities in every way possible. Working together to achieve these goals and help our fellow men creates an unshakable bond.


Our Mission

Local causes of the Lodge include volunteering for the Houston Food Bank and participating in the restoration and maintenance of Olivewood Cemetery working with the 501(c)3 Descendants of Olivewood, Inc. The Lodge is always seeking input for new volunteer opportunities, with an eye towards Heights area non-profits and service organizations. In addition, the Lodge pays dues to the National Association of Odd Fellows supporting numerous charitable efforts and humanitarian endeavors such as scholarships, student loans and grants, nursing homes for the elderly, family and youth summer camps and annual donations to charities.

Our lodge also helps support many activities internationally, the Independent Order of Odd Fellows are involved in numerous charitable efforts and humanitarian endeavors such as scholarships, low-interest student loans and grants, low-cost nursing homes for the elderly, family and youth summer camps and annual donations to charities.
